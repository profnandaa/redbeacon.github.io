The title would be Hello "World" if we weren't aiming higher

Making the simple complicated is commonplace; making the complicated simple, awesomely simple, that's creativity.

Charles Mingus

Great news folks! We've decided to create a tech blog for spreading our passion and love for making incredible apps with anyone who is interested in building great products. Our engineers, designers and product managers are eager and excited to share their ideas and experiences.

Speaking of our engineers, we think this is a great opportunity for contributing back, as we mainly use open-source software for building our apps.

Since this is our first blog post, let us take you on a short tour to see around.

Wait, what is Redbeacon again?

Remember Dr. Who's TARDIS? If not, it's just a simple box from outside that is actually a time machine and spacecraft that is way bigger from the inside, which is pretty much like Redbeacon.

In case you've landed on this page wondering what we do: Redbeacon is an awesomely simple product built by a bunch of talented and creative people working smart for changing the way people find professionals they need to make their homes better.

Illustrations are always better than words:

You might ask, "so Redbeacon is a place where you get pros to clean your home?" It's not just cleaning but every home-related task you need.

It's really fun to tell what your home needs since our legendary request service tool understands simple sentences like:

  • Unclog my sink
  • Paint my living room
  • Fix my roof
  • Set up my new sound system
  • Upgrade my electrical wiring

    • Go ahead and try some of the things your home needs!

After you request a service, our magic matching engine sends you up to 4 competitive quotes by pros whose reputation are confirmed with customer reviews and previous work history.

Oh, by the way, if you're only interested in learning how much the job will cost, we've built an intelligent price estimator tool for that, too.

I know kung fu. What should I expect from this blog?

Our engineering team have lots of things to share: best practices, code snippets, failures, experiments, optimizations, statistics, problems and solutions... The major percentage of our codebase is Python / Django. We would use Python to make our coffee if we could, seriously. So expect to see a lot of Python here. Our frontend on the other hand is mostly Backbone.js and we've created some cool extensions that we would love to share.

Numbers are always fun (nerd alert). I've put some numbers together for seeing the big picture even clearer.

Having developers outside the US gives us the advantage of receiving commits every hour of the day, generating this incredible punchcard.

We are growing fast, and our codebase is expanding at blazing speeds and handle most of the introduced bugs with our tests. We have Hacking Fridays completely dedicated to bug fixing, unit tests and selenium tests. Hacking Fridays are really fun and challenging; engineers are divided into several teams and there is an insane competition where we hold a record for fixing 152 bugs in one day!
